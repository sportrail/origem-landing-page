The FORAY Difference

9 Months.
2 Countries.
2 Football Cultures.

Most gap year programmes put you in one place. FORAY puts you in two — alternating between Portugal’s technical, possession-based football and Northern Ireland’s physical, direct game. You don’t just develop as a player. You develop as a complete footballer who can adapt to any style, anywhere.

“The players who arrive to college tryouts with experience in two European football cultures don’t just stand out. They stand alone.”

This structure also solves the visa challenge: by alternating between the Schengen Area (Portugal) and the UK (Northern Ireland), non-EU athletes stay within the 90-day visa-free limits — no special visa required.

Train and compete in the Portuguese league system, known worldwide for producing the most technically complete players on earth. The same development culture that shaped Cristiano Ronaldo, Bernardo Silva, and Bruno Fernandes.

🇬🇧
Glentoran FC
NIFL Premiership · Founded 1882

One of Northern Ireland’s most historic and successful clubs. Glentoran compete in the NIFL Premiership — the top tier of Northern Irish football. A club with European pedigree, passionate supporters, and a winning culture at The Oval, East Belfast.

In the 1967/68 European Cup, it was a Glentoran–Benfica tie that helped establish the away goals rule in European football — a rule that shaped the Champions League for over fifty years. The connection between these two footballing nations runs deep. FORAY is built on it.

Smart Structure

No Special Visa Required.

US citizens can stay up to 90 days visa-free in the Schengen Area (Portugal) and separately in the UK (Northern Ireland). By alternating between both, you complete the full 9-month programme without ever exceeding either limit.

Schengen rules allow 90 days within any 180-day period. The Northern Ireland phase resets the clock. No E5 visa or student visa needed for US passport holders.

Eligibility Protected

NCAA Compliant. Guaranteed.

FORAY is specifically designed around NCAA Bylaw 12.2.3.2.1 — the pre-enrollment exception. Your four seasons of eligibility remain untouched.

01
Zero Compensation

You receive meals, housing, equipment, transport, and medical care — nothing more. No salary, no stipends, no bonuses. Only “actual and necessary expenses” as defined by NCAA Bylaw 12.02.2.

02
No Professional Contract

You are an amateur athlete in a development programme. No document will ever classify you as professional or semi-professional. In either country.

03
12-Month Grace Period

Our 9-month programme ends well within the NCAA’s 12-month delayed enrollment window (Bylaw 14.2.4). Your eligibility clock doesn’t start until you enroll in college.

We provide a full NCAA Compliance Statement for your college’s athletics department. Division I, II, and III eligible. NAIA athletes should contact us for specific guidance.
